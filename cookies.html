Cookie Policy

Last Updated: June 15, 2023

Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how YumWhirl ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy. Cookies are small data files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the website owners.

Types of Cookies We Use

1. Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for you to browse the website and use its features, such as accessing secure areas of the site. Without these cookies, services like shopping baskets or e-billing cannot be provided. They are usually set in response to actions made by you such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information and cannot be disabled in our systems.

2. Performance Cookies

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us to know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. If you do not allow these cookies we will not know when you have visited our site and will not be able to monitor its performance and make improvements.

3. Functional Cookies

These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages. If you do not allow these cookies then some or all of these services may not function properly. These cookies remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or region) to provide enhanced, more personal features and to save you time by eliminating the need to re-enter preferences.

4. Targeting Cookies

These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant advertisements on other sites. They do not store direct personal information but are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising. These cookies track visitors across websites to display ads that are more relevant and engaging for users.

5. Social Media Cookies

These cookies are set by a range of social media services that we have added to the site to enable you to share our content with your friends and networks. They are capable of tracking your browser across other sites and building up a profile of your interests. This may impact the content and messages you see on other websites you visit. If you do not allow these cookies you may not be able to use or see these sharing tools.

6. First-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set by the website you are visiting, and they can only be read by that site. We use first-party cookies for various functions including session management, authentication, and preference storage. These cookies are generally considered to be more secure and less invasive than third-party cookies as they are directly controlled by us and not shared with external domains.

7. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are set by domains other than the one you are visiting, typically by advertisers and analytics companies. These cookies are used for cross-site tracking, retargeting, and ad-serving purposes. We carefully vet all third-party services that set cookies through our site to ensure they comply with privacy regulations and our own standards for data protection.

8. Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary cookies that remain on your device until you leave the website. These are essential for proper functioning of our website, particularly for maintaining your session and ensuring security during your visit. They enable features like maintaining your logged-in status as you navigate between pages. Session cookies are automatically deleted when you close your browser.

9. Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period specified in the cookie (usually between a few days to several years) unless you delete them manually. We use persistent cookies to remember your preferences and settings for future visits, analyze long-term user behavior patterns, and provide personalized content. The lifespan of each persistent cookie varies depending on its purpose.

10. Flash Cookies

Flash cookies (also known as Local Shared Objects) are data files similar to cookies that are used by Adobe Flash Player. They can store more complex data than regular cookies and are not managed through your browser settings. We may use Flash cookies to remember settings, preferences, and usage for media content. These can be managed through the Adobe Flash Player Settings Manager.

Purposes of Cookie Usage

11. Website Functionality

We use cookies to enable basic functions like page navigation and access to secure areas of the website. These cookies are essential for the website to function properly and cannot be switched off in our systems. They include features such as maintaining your logged-in status, remembering items in your shopping cart, and ensuring secure checkout processes. Without these cookies, core functionality of our site would be impaired or non-functional.

12. Performance Monitoring

Cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This includes tracking which pages are most frequently visited, how users navigate between sections, and identifying any technical issues. Performance cookies provide metrics that help us optimize site architecture, improve content delivery, and enhance overall user experience without collecting personally identifiable information.

13. Personalization

We use cookies to remember information about your preferences and customize your experience accordingly. This includes language settings, dietary preferences you've indicated, recently viewed recipes, and layout choices. Personalization cookies enable features like recommending recipes based on your browsing history and maintaining your preferred viewing settings across visits to create a more tailored experience.

14. Advertising

Cookies help us deliver relevant advertisements to you and measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns. They track whether you've seen an ad, how you interacted with it, and prevent you from seeing the same ad too many times. Advertising cookies also enable us to show you personalized content recommendations and special offers based on your browsing behavior both on and off our site.

15. Social Media Integration

Cookies enable you to share content directly to social media platforms and allow social media services to recognize you when you view social media content on our website. These cookies may track your browser across other sites to build a profile of your interests. Social media cookies make it possible to like or share content, follow our social media accounts directly from our site, and display personalized feeds of our social content.

16. Security

We use security cookies to authenticate users, prevent fraudulent use of login credentials, and protect user data from unauthorized access. These cookies help identify and mitigate potential security risks by detecting unusual patterns or multiple failed login attempts. Security cookies are essential for protecting your account and ensuring that no one else can access your personal information without authorization.

17. A/B Testing

Cookies allow us to test different versions of our website to determine which design and content performs best. A/B testing cookies ensure you consistently see the same version of a page during your session and help us collect data about user engagement with different layouts. This information helps us make data-driven decisions about site improvements while maintaining a consistent experience for individual users.

18. Affiliate Tracking

We use cookies to track referrals from affiliate partners and properly credit them for any resulting subscriptions or purchases. These cookies record which affiliate referred you to our site and may track your journey through the site to determine if you complete a qualifying action. Affiliate cookies help ensure our partners are fairly compensated while maintaining transparency about the referral process.

19. Location-Based Services

With your permission, cookies can help us provide location-specific content such as regional recipes, local ingredient availability, and time-appropriate meal suggestions. Location cookies may determine your approximate position based on IP address or more precise location if you enable GPS features. This allows us to customize content based on your geographic location, climate, and local culinary traditions.

20. Analytics Integration

We use cookies to integrate with third-party analytics services that help us understand how our website is used. These services collect information such as how often users visit the site, what pages they visit, and what other sites they used prior to coming to our site. Analytics cookies provide aggregated data that helps us improve our content strategy and technical performance without identifying individual visitors.

Cookie Management

21. Browser Cookie Controls

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. You can set your browser to refuse all or some cookies, or to alert you when websites set or access cookies. However, if you disable or refuse cookies, please note that some parts of our website may become inaccessible or not function properly. Each browser is different, so check the "Help" menu of your browser to learn how to modify your cookie settings.

22. Cookie Consent Tool

We provide a cookie consent management tool that allows you to customize your cookie preferences. This tool appears when you first visit our site and can be accessed at any time through a link in the footer. Through this interface, you can enable or disable different categories of cookies (except strictly necessary ones) and see detailed information about each cookie we use.

23. Opting Out of Advertising Cookies

You can opt out of targeted advertising by visiting the Digital Advertising Alliance's opt-out portal or the Network Advertising Initiative's opt-out tool. These industry-developed programs allow you to reject targeting cookies from many major ad networks. Note that opting out doesn't mean you won't see ads - it means the ads you see may be less relevant to your interests.

24. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers incorporate a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites you visit that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Currently, our systems do not recognize browser "Do Not Track" signals as there is no industry standard for implementation. However, we provide you with alternative control options through our cookie consent tool and privacy settings.

25. Mobile Device Controls

On mobile devices, you may have additional controls over cookies through your device settings. Both iOS and Android provide options to limit ad tracking and reset advertising identifiers. These controls vary by device manufacturer and operating system version. Consult your device documentation for specific instructions on managing cookies and tracking technologies in mobile browsers and apps.

26. Flash Cookie Management

Flash cookies are managed differently than regular browser cookies through the Adobe Flash Player Settings Manager. This manager allows you to control which websites can store information on your computer, delete specific Flash cookies, or prevent Flash content from storing data altogether. The Settings Manager is available on Adobe's website and must be accessed separately from your browser settings.

27. Periodic Cookie Review

We recommend periodically reviewing and cleaning the cookies stored on your devices. Most browsers allow you to see all cookies currently stored and selectively delete them. Regular cookie maintenance helps protect your privacy and can improve browser performance. Instructions for accessing cookie storage vary by browser but are typically found in privacy or history settings.

28. Third-Party Opt-Outs

Many third-party services we use offer their own opt-out mechanisms for their tracking technologies. For example, Google Analytics provides a browser add-on to opt out of their analytics tracking. We maintain a list of these third-party opt-out options in our privacy center. These opt-outs are specific to each provider and may require separate actions beyond our cookie consent tool.

29. Cookie Expiration

Different cookies have different lifespans. Session cookies expire when you close your browser, while persistent cookies remain for a set period (from days to years). You can see the expiration date of each cookie in your browser's cookie storage. We regularly review and update cookie expiration periods to balance functionality needs with privacy considerations.

30. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Disabling cookies may affect your ability to use certain features of our website. Essential functions like logging in, adding items to your cart, and making purchases may not work without strictly necessary cookies. Disabling performance cookies means we won't be able to gather information to improve user experience. Personalization features and saved preferences will also be unavailable without functional cookies.

Additional Information

31. Cookie Policy Updates

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. When we make changes, we will revise the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy. We encourage you to periodically review this page for the latest information on our cookie practices. Significant changes will be communicated through prominent notices on our website.

32. Legal Compliance

Our cookie practices comply with applicable data protection laws including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). We provide clear information about cookie usage, obtain consent where required, and offer granular control options. Our data processing agreements with third-party cookie providers ensure they meet the same compliance standards we adhere to.

33. Children's Privacy

We do not knowingly use cookies to collect personal information from children under 13 without parental consent. Our website is not directed to children, and we take special precautions regarding cookies for any child-directed sections. Parents can contact us to review any information collected from their child and request deletion of such information and associated cookies.

34. International Data Transfers

Some third-party cookie providers may process data outside your country of residence. When this occurs, we implement appropriate safeguards as required by applicable laws. For transfers from the European Economic Area, we ensure providers participate in the EU-U.S. Privacy Shield Framework or implement Standard Contractual Clauses to provide adequate protection for transferred data.

35. Data Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect cookie data against unauthorized access, alteration, or destruction. This includes encryption of sensitive data, regular security assessments of our systems, and limiting access to cookie data to authorized personnel only. Our security practices are regularly reviewed and updated to address emerging threats.

36. Cookie Audits

We conduct regular audits of the cookies used on our website to ensure they remain necessary for their stated purposes. These audits review cookie functionality, lifespan, data collection practices, and third-party access. Based on audit findings, we may remove unnecessary cookies, update cookie settings, or change service providers to better protect user privacy.

37. Data Retention Periods

Cookie data is retained only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy unless a longer retention period is required by law. Retention periods vary by cookie type and purpose. Analytics data may be kept for up to 26 months, while advertising cookies typically have shorter lifespans. Personal data collected through cookies is subject to our Privacy Policy retention schedules.

38. Cookie Lists

We maintain comprehensive lists of all cookies used on our website, including their purpose, duration, and whether they are first-party or third-party cookies. These lists are available through our cookie consent tool and are regularly updated as we add or remove cookies. The lists provide transparency about exactly what tracking technologies are active when you use our site.

39. Contact Information

If you have questions about our use of cookies or this policy, please contact our Data Protection Officer at privacy@yumwhirl.com or at our postal address. For technical questions about managing cookies on your devices, you may contact our support team at help@yumwhirl.com. We aim to respond to all cookie-related inquiries within 5 business days.

40. Related Policies

This Cookie Policy should be read in conjunction with our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service, which provide additional information about how we collect, use, and protect personal data. Our Privacy Policy explains your rights regarding personal information collected through cookies, while our Terms of Service govern your use of our website and services.
